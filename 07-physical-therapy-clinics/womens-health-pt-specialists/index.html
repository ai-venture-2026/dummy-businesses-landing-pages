Conditions We Treat

Specialized Care for Women's Health

We provide evidence-based physical therapy for conditions that are often under-addressed — in a private, judgment-free environment.

Pelvic Floor Dysfunction

Hypertonic and hypotonic pelvic floor conditions, chronic pelvic pain, and musculoskeletal imbalances affecting the pelvic girdle. Manual therapy, biofeedback, and therapeutic exercise.

Urinary Incontinence

Stress, urge, and mixed urinary incontinence treated through pelvic floor muscle retraining, bladder behavioral strategies, and targeted neuromuscular reeducation.

Diastasis Recti

Assessment and rehabilitation of abdominal wall separation, including linea alba integrity restoration, core pressure management, and functional movement reintegration.

Prenatal Discomfort

Management of pregnancy-related musculoskeletal pain including symphysis pubis dysfunction, sacroiliac joint pain, round ligament discomfort, and postural adaptations across all trimesters.

Postpartum Recovery

Comprehensive postpartum rehabilitation addressing perineal healing, scar tissue mobility, pelvic organ prolapse, core restoration, and return-to-activity progressions after vaginal and cesarean delivery.

Painful Intercourse

Treatment of dyspareunia and vaginismus through pelvic floor desensitization, soft tissue mobilization, dilator therapy education, and neuromuscular re-education in a sensitive, private clinical environment.

Prenatal & Postpartum Care

Supporting You Through Every Stage of Motherhood

From the first trimester through the fourth, we provide targeted physical therapy that evolves with your body's changing needs.

First Trimester: Building a Strong Foundation

Early pregnancy is an ideal time to establish pelvic floor awareness and address any pre-existing musculoskeletal issues before they are amplified by the demands of a growing pregnancy.

  • Pelvic floor baseline assessment and education
  • Addressing pre-existing diastasis recti or pelvic pain
  • Safe core and hip stabilization programming
  • Postural assessment and ergonomic guidance
  • Bladder health and pelvic hygiene education
Book a Prenatal Consultation

Second Trimester: Managing Growth-Related Changes

As your center of gravity shifts and ligaments relax, the second trimester often brings new musculoskeletal challenges. We adapt your plan as your body changes week to week.

  • Sacroiliac joint and symphysis pubis dysfunction management
  • Progesterone-related ligament laxity stabilization
  • Round ligament and hip flexor tension relief
  • Posture correction and gait training
  • Diastasis recti monitoring and load management
Book a Prenatal Consultation

Third Trimester: Preparing for Birth

The third trimester focuses on labor preparation, pain management, and positioning strategies to support optimal fetal positioning and a smoother delivery experience.

  • Pelvic floor preparation and labor positioning guidance
  • Perineal massage technique instruction
  • Sciatic nerve and low back pain management
  • Carpal tunnel and upper extremity swelling treatment
  • Postpartum recovery planning and goal setting
Book a Prenatal Consultation

Postpartum: Your Fourth Trimester

Recovery after childbirth — whether vaginal or cesarean — deserves the same clinical attention as any surgical recovery. We guide you through a safe, evidence-based return to function and activity.

  • Pelvic floor function assessment (safe at 6 weeks postpartum)
  • Perineal scar tissue and episiotomy healing
  • Cesarean section scar mobilization
  • Diastasis recti rehab and abdominal wall restoration
  • Graduated return to exercise, running, and sport

Your First Visit

What to Expect

We know that coming in for pelvic floor therapy can feel vulnerable. Here is exactly what your first appointment will look like — no surprises.

1

Private Welcome

You arrive through our discreet private entrance and are greeted by a female staff member. No shared waiting area with other specialties.

2

Thorough Intake

Your therapist spends time reviewing your history, understanding your goals, and answering every question in a private consultation room.

3

Consent-Led Assessment

Every component of the physical assessment is explained before it is performed. Internal assessments are always optional and performed only with your explicit, informed consent.

4

Personalized Plan

You leave with a clear, written treatment plan including your diagnosis, goals, expected timeline, and home exercises — in plain language.

5

Ongoing Support

Your therapist is available by secure message between sessions. Progress is monitored at every visit and your plan is updated as you improve.

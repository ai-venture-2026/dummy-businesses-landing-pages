Urgency Guide

When to Come In Immediately

If your pet is showing any of the following signs, call us immediately and come in right away. Every minute counts in a true emergency.

Difficulty Breathing

Labored breathing, gasping, wheezing, choking, or blue-tinged gums — a life-threatening emergency requiring immediate care.

Seizures

Any seizure activity, prolonged convulsions, or multiple seizures within 24 hours requires emergency neurological evaluation.

Trauma or Injury

Hit by a vehicle, fall from height, animal attack, or suspected internal injuries require urgent evaluation even if your pet seems stable.

Suspected Poisoning

Known or suspected ingestion of toxins — plants, medications, household chemicals, or food hazards. Do not wait for symptoms.

Uncontrolled Bleeding

Deep lacerations, wounds that won't stop bleeding, blood in urine or stool, or bloody vomit are urgent warning signs.

Collapse or Weakness

Sudden collapse, inability to stand, extreme lethargy, unconsciousness, or rapid decline in consciousness is a critical emergency.

Advanced Emergency Care

We provide the full spectrum of emergency and critical care services — available every hour of every day.

Emergency Surgery

Immediate surgical intervention for life-threatening conditions. Our board-certified surgeons are on call 24/7 for GDV, foreign body obstruction, ruptured organs, and more.

Critical Care ICU

Around-the-clock intensive monitoring in our advanced ICU. Continuous vital sign surveillance, oxygen therapy, IV support, and dedicated nursing care.

Toxicology & Poison Control

Expert management of poisoning and toxic exposures. We work with poison control specialists and maintain antidotes for the most common pet toxins.

Trauma Care

Rapid response trauma stabilization for injuries from accidents, falls, and animal attacks. Advanced pain management and life-support from the moment you walk in.

Diagnostic Imaging

In-house digital X-ray, ultrasound, and CT scanning capabilities for fast, accurate diagnosis. Results available within minutes — not hours.

Blood Transfusions

Life-saving blood product therapy for severe anemia, hemorrhage, and clotting disorders. We maintain an on-site blood bank for dogs and cats.

Emergency Protocol

What to Do in an Emergency

Follow these three steps when your pet has a medical emergency. Time is critical — do not wait to see if your pet improves on their own.

1

Stay Calm

Keep yourself and your pet as calm as possible. Avoid restraining an injured animal forcefully — this can worsen injuries and increase stress. Speak in a low, reassuring voice.

2

Call Us Now

Call ahead so our team can prepare for your arrival. We will give you immediate guidance over the phone and have a team ready when you walk in.

(555) 123-4567
3

Come Immediately

Drive directly to our hospital. Park in any of the clearly marked emergency spaces at the entrance. A technician will meet you at the door. We are open 24 hours, 365 days a year.
